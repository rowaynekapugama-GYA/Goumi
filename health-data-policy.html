About this policy

This Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy applies specifically to "consumer health data" as defined under the Washington My Health My Data Act (MHMDA) and the equivalent Nevada law (SB 370). It is provided as a standalone policy, separate from our general Privacy Policy, and is linked from our homepage. If you are a consumer in Washington or Nevada, this policy describes how we collect, use, share, and protect your consumer health data, and the rights you have.

This policy is operated by [goumi Pty Ltd] ("goumi", "we", "us"). Questions: [privacy@goumi.app].

1. What we mean by consumer health data

Consumer health data is personal information linked to you that identifies your past, present, or future physical or mental health status. For goumi, this means the information you choose to share so we can tailor recipes to you, including:

  • allergies and food intolerances (for example, nut, gluten, or dairy allergies);
  • dietary restrictions and preferences tied to health (for example, low-sodium, diabetic-friendly, low-carb);
  • health and nutrition goals (for example, weight management, muscle gain, managing a condition through diet); and
  • inferences we draw from the above to personalise recipes and nutritional estimates.

We do not collect biometric data, precise location for health purposes, or data about reproductive or sexual health.

2. How we collect it and our sources

We collect consumer health data directly from you when you choose to enter it into the app (for example, when setting up dietary preferences or allergy filters). We do not buy consumer health data, and we do not collect it from data brokers.

3. How we use it

We use your consumer health data only to:

  • personalise the recipes, meal plans, and nutritional estimates we show you;
  • apply your allergy and dietary filters; and
  • provide the features you have asked for.

We do not use consumer health data for advertising, and we do not profile you for marketing.

4. How we share it

We do not sell your consumer health data. We will not sell it without the separate, valid authorisation the law requires, and we have no plans to sell it.

We keep your consumer health data within our Australian cloud environment (AWS Sydney) and limit access to it. We share it with a service provider (processor) only where necessary to provide the Service to you and under a contract that restricts their use of it. We do not share consumer health data with overseas AI or voice providers unless it is necessary to deliver a feature you have requested, and we minimise what is shared. We may also disclose it where required by law.

The categories of recipients with whom we may share data to operate goumi are listed in our general Privacy Policy. You can request a specific list of the third parties and affiliates with whom we have shared your consumer health data (see section 7).

5. How we obtain your consent

Before we collect or use your consumer health data for any purpose beyond what is strictly necessary to provide a feature you have requested, we ask for your clear, affirmative, opt-in consent, presented separately from our other terms. You are free to decline, and you can withdraw your consent at any time (see section 7), after which we will stop collecting and using that data and will delete it.

6. Data security and retention

We protect consumer health data with encryption in transit and at rest, access controls, and restricted internal access. We retain it only for as long as needed to provide the Service or as required by law, and we delete it when you withdraw consent or delete your account.

7. Your rights

If you are a Washington or Nevada consumer, you have the right to:

  • Confirm and access — confirm whether we are collecting, sharing, or selling your consumer health data, and access that data;
  • List of recipients — obtain a list of the third parties and affiliates with whom we have shared or to whom we have sold your consumer health data;
  • Delete — request deletion of your consumer health data; and
  • Withdraw consent — withdraw your consent to our collection and sharing of your consumer health data.

To exercise any of these rights, contact [privacy@goumi.app] or use the controls in the app's privacy settings. We will respond within 45 days (extendable by a further 45 days where reasonably necessary, with notice to you). We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights. We may need to verify your identity before acting on a request.

8. Appeals and complaints

If we decline your request, you may appeal by replying to our response or contacting [privacy@goumi.app]. If your appeal is denied, you may contact the Washington State Attorney General at atg.wa.gov/file-complaint (Washington consumers) or the Nevada Attorney General (Nevada consumers).

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time and will post the updated version with a new "Last updated" date.
